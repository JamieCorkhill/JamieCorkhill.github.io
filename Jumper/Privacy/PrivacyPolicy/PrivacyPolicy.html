3.1 Google Analytics for Firebase

Google Analytics for Firebase is “a free app measurement solution that provides insight on app usage and user engagement”. In the course of its normal operation, and unless consent is otherwise denied by the user, Jumper uses Google Analytics for Firebase to monitor how the application is performing and functioning, and to collect anonymous usage statistics that will allow us to improve our Products and Services.

As an example, it would allow us to see how many people are using the app, view retention rates, understand how many users are moving to an updated version of the application, determine if the application is too hard or too easy (through transmission of your score), etc., via many different events that it collects, including one’s that we define. Google Analytics lets us aggregate these events in many ways.

By default, and where applicable, Google Analytics for Firebase collects various anonymous information, such as your age bracket, gender, interests, etc., as well as the language used on your device, the store from which you downloaded and installed the application, your device brand, category, and model, etc., a full list of which may be found here (https://support.google.com/analytics/answer/6317486?hl=en).

Google Analytics for Firebase transfers five items of personal data from your device to their systems, likely outside the EU:
  1. Mobile Advertising ID
  2. IDFVs/Android IDs
  3. Instance IDs
  4. Analytics App Instance IDs
  5. IP Address
In the sections below, we describe the meaning of these terms, and the reasons for which they are used.

3.1.a Mobile Advertsing ID

The Mobile Advertising ID is an alphanumeric sequence, or string, representing a hexadecimal number.

Unlike websites, mobile applications do not use Cookies. Instead, they rely on Mobile Advertising Identifiers. Typically, this ID is called an “Android Advertising ID”, or AdID, on Android devices, and an “Identifier for Advertisers”, or IDFA, on Apple devices.

Mobile In-app Advertising IDs allow developers and marketers to track activity for advertising purposes. They’re also used to enhance serving and targeting capabilities.

In Jumper’s operation, we don’t serve personalized ads, and subsequently, Mobile Advertising IDs are not used by our Ad Networks for that purpose. Instead, Mobile Ad IDs are used by Google Analytics for Firebase to determine a user’s demographics, interests, and approximate geo-location. In the case of Android, the Android Advertising ID is an “anonymous identifier for advertising purposes and enables users to reset their identifier or opt out of interest-based ads within Google Play apps.”

It is based off of this ID that Google derives the aforementioned data, such as your age bracket, gender, device information, etc.

3.1.b IDFVs/Android ID

The IDFVs (Identifier for Vendors), for Apple, is an alphanumeric string that is unique across devices.

The Android ID is a 64-bit number, expressed as a hexadecimal string, that is unique to each combination of the app signing key, the user, and the device. It is used as a unique device identifier.

3.1.c Instance ID

The Instance ID is a unique identifier that identifies individual installations of an application and provides Google Firebase Services a method by which to refer to specific app instances. You can read more about Instance IDs here (https://developers.google.com/instance-id/).

Google Analytics for Firebase uses Instance IDs to track particular audiences. Data associated with Instance IDs is not generally considered personally identifiable.

This Instance ID is also used by our crash reporting service Crashlytics, which is discussed later.

3.1.d Analytics App Instance ID

The Analytics App Instance ID is hexadecimal string that specifies you as a distinct user within Google Firebase, for it is unique to your installation of Jumper. It remains unchanged except when the app is restored onto a new device, the app is uninstalled and then reinstalled, or you clear app data.

3.1.e Internet Protocol (IP) Address

Google Analytics for Firebase collects your IP Address to derive or predict your location. It’s important to note that your IP Address never reaches Google’s systems, and as such, we, the data-controller, do not have access to view it, nor is it provided in our reports. Additionally, your IP Address is fully anonymized by Google at the earliest possible time. This is done to comply with relevant Internet privacy laws.

We could use this information, for example, to view how many people in the United States were able to achieve a particular score, or how many people in the United Kingdom played the game for more than 10 minutes a day.

3.1.f Why the Data is Collected

Jumper, and subsequently, we, partner with various third-party services such as Google Firebase to collect anonymous usage statistics about the function and performance of the application in order to improve it.

Google Analytics uses this data to provide analytics and attribution information. The precise information collected can vary by the device and environment. For more information, see https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites.

3.1.g Collected Data Retention Period

Google Analytics retains certain advertising identifier associated data (e.g., Apple’s Identifier for Advertisers and Identifier for Vendors, Android’s Advertising ID) for 60 days, and retains aggregate reporting and certain user-level campaign data without automatic expiration.

3.1.h Data Protection and Collection

3.2 Fabric Crashlytics

Fabric is a mobile app developer platform that has been acquired by Google LLC. Crashlytics is a crash-reporting tool, now owned by Google, that provides developers with information pertaining to crashes and, when partnered with Google Analytics for Firebase, the events that may have caused said crash.

Fabric Crashlytics, a subsidiary of Google LLC, transfers three items of data from your device to their systems, likely outside the EU:
  1. Installation UUID (IDFVs or Android ID)
  2. IP Address
  3. Crash Data
The Installation UUID, as described above in section 3.1.b, is a 128-bit number that allows us to uniquely identify your crash.

3.2.b Internet Protocol (IP) Address

Fabric Crashlytics, a subsidiary of Google LLC, collects IP Addresses which it geocodes to a city, and which is retained on the Audience Insights map for 10 seconds.

3.2.c Crash Data

In the event that the Jumper application experiences an unexpected crash, we collect specific crash report and device data through Fabric, a subsidiary of Google LLC, specifically Fabric Crashlytics. The following anonymous and non-personally identifiable information is collected:
  1. Various technical information related to the crash (errors, stack traces, etc.).
  2. Timestamp (a Unix Timestamp representing the number of seconds since January 1, 1970).
  3. App Identifier.
  4. Device Operating System name and version.
  5. CPU Architecture.
  6. RAM and Disk Space.
  7. And more (https://try.crashlytics.com/security/).

3.2.d Why the Data is Collected

Jumper partners with various third-party services such as Fabric to attain anonymous usage statistics, crash reporting data, and performance metrics. This allows us to better improve our Products and Services. The aforementioned data provides us with analytics information based on segmented device data. IP Addresses provide us with geolocation information.

3.2.e Collected Data Retention Period

Fabric retains Installation UUID data for a period of 90 days.

3.2.f Data Protection and Collection

You can learn more about how Fabric, a subsidiary of Google LLC, collects, retains, and protects your data here (https://docs.fabric.io/apple/fabric/data-privacy.html#data-collection-policies) and here (https://fabric.io/terms).
